Consolidation

Most theft incidents that happen to PC Volunteers are pickpocketing and take place during travel. Majority of cases happen in Kyiv metro and in/near Central Train Station.

Main mitigation measure is to use situational awareness and remember, that someone is a suitable target for a theft, if they:
* are distracted;
* openly display money or valuables;
* are intoxicated;
* leave their belongings unattended;
* are in the large crowd.

In case theft happened:

  1. Report to SSM exactly what was stolen: cash, bank cards, property (cell phone, digital camera, etc.) and the circumstances of the theft: place, method used and if there are any suspects.
  2. Consider taking some actions for recovering the property. For instance, if the purse/wallet disappeared in a café, Volunteer might try to go there and ask the personnel if they found it.
  3. If bank cards were stolen, call 24-hour bank card service center in Ukraine and the US bank depending on the cards origin and block them ASAP.
  4. If passport and/or registration document “posvidka” are stolen, police report must be filed.
  5. If PCV loses a property that is covered with US insurance police report is required for claiming insurance reimbursement.
